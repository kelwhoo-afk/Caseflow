# BayBridge Auto Injury — Referral Consent and Confidentiality Policy

## Confidential information

The following information must be treated as confidential and should not be shared with a partner firm until client consent is granted:

- Caller name
- Phone number
- Email
- Accident location/date
- Injury description
- Medical treatment details
- Insurance details
- Transcript excerpts
- Photos, documents, police report information, or witness details

## Consent prompt

"Based on what you shared, this matter may be a better fit for one of our trusted partner firms, [Receiving Firm]. Your information is confidential, and we will not share your case summary or contact information without your permission. Do you consent to us sharing your intake details with [Receiving Firm] so they can review whether they may be able to help?"

## Consent outcomes

### If consent is granted
- Mark consent status: granted.
- Unlock referral packet.
- Send packet to receiving firm.
- Notify client that packet has been sent.
- Create referral tracking record.

### If consent is denied
- Mark consent status: denied.
- Keep packet locked.
- Do not share externally.
- Offer a general resource response or ask whether the client wants more information.

### If client wants more information
- Provide partner firm name, coverage, why it may be a fit, and what information would be shared.
- Ask again for consent before sharing.

## Audit fields

- Consent status
- Timestamp
- Receiving firm
- Information categories shared
- User approving referral
- Packet sent status
